<h1>Hacker School is a writers&rsquo; retreat for programmers.</h1>
<p>
Want to spend three months coding and learning in New York with smart, friendly, and intellectually curious people? <a href="/about">Read on</a>.
</p>
</div>
<div id='banner'>
<p>New blog post: <a href="/blog/33-summer-and-fall-2014-applications-are-open">Summer and fall 2014 applications are open</a></p>
</div>
<div id='tenets'>
<h2>Hacker School is...</h2>
<ul>
<li>
<h3>Self-directed</h3>
<p>We value intrinsic motivation and self-direction, and believe people learn best when they're free to explore their passions and interests.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Accessible</h3>
<p><strong>We're free for all students.</strong> We also offer $5,000 need-based grants for living expenses for female programmers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Stimulating</h3>
<p>We look for smart, friendly, self-directed, and intellectually curious people who enjoy programming and want to get dramatically better.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Exceptional</h3>
<p>We have exceptional residents including Peter Norvig, Jessica McKellar, Yaron Minsky, David Nolen, Peter Seibel, Nada Amin, and more.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Thoughtful</h3>
<p>The atmosphere here is friendly and intellectual. We have <strong>a gender-balanced environment</strong>, and lightweight social rules.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Supportive</h3>
<p>We have a tight-knit community of nearly <strong>300 alumni across 24 countries.</strong> Our motto is "never graduate."</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Project-based</h3>
<p>Hacker Schoolers have made significant contributions to dozens of open source projects and started many of their own.</p>
</li>
<li>
<h3>Connected</h3>
<p>We make money by helping great companies hire our alumni. There's no obligation to take a job if you don't want one.</p>
</li>
</ul>
